    The Beach House is a stunning coastal property of superior quality situated in an unrivalled beach front position with direct access onto Doonin strand and offering magnificent, uninterrupted sea and coastal views. Immediately it becomes obvious as you walk towards the entrance to the property how special this setting and position really is, as the property enjoys breathtaking views over the Atlantic Ocean and Donegal Bay which are particularly special at every time of the day and in every season. Built in 2005 to maximise its glorious sea views, this architecturally and sympathetically designed luxury family residence extends to approximately 5,240 sq.ft (487 sq.m) standing on a south facing site with around 70 meters of shoreline frontage on circa 1.8 acres (0.70 ha). Of particular feature is the generously proportioned ground floor reception room containing a 19ft double height vaulted ceiling with exposed roof trusses provides an ideal open space for family time, to socialise, relax and cook whilst enjoying the unhindered views. The entire property is beautifully appointed throughout with high quality fittings and elegantly appointed modern d\xc3\xa9cor, the property is generously proportioned with the accommodation flowing attractively from room to room.\r\n\r\nLocation:\r\nCo. Donegal is one of the most beautiful counties of Ireland, offering unrivalled scenery with its many\r\nspectacular land and seascapes. The Atlantic shoreline is etched with a multitude of beautiful bays and glorious sandy beaches. Overlooking much of this coastline are the rugged mountains and hills, stretching from the unique Mount Errigal in the north of the county, to the foreboding beauty of the aptly named Blue Stack Mountains in the south. \r\n\r\nKilcar has become known for its picturesque scenery where the Atlantic meets the rolling hills of Donegal. The Wild Atlantic Way on Ireland's west coast leads you through one of the world's most dramatic coastal landscapes, a landscape on the edge of Europe that has shaped the development of its people, communities and settlements, a landscape that has inspired its own particular language, literature, art, song and dance. It's a place of many natural features - seascapes, sea-life, cliffs, mountains, glens, loughs, trails and pathways. It's a place to experience nature at its wildest, a place to explore the history of the Gaels and their religion, a place to experience great events, great food and drink, great music and the craic.\r\n\r\nDonegal Bay majestically swoops towards you, while to the right, sits the majestic Sliabh Liag, Europe's highest accessible sea cliffs and the huge panoramas presented to you from these cliffs can look different in every segment. For the experienced walker One Man's pass will take you to the summit of Sliabh Liag for the leisure visitor the viewing platform will be all you need.
