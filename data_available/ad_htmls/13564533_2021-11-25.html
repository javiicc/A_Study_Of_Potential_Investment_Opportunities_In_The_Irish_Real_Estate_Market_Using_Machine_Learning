    Maher Auctioneers are delighted to welcome Hill House to the market. Hill House is a graciously proportioned family home which is beautifully presented with a mature fantastically maintained garden and useful outbuilding. The house has been refurbished to the highest standard over the last eight years by the present owners. The property is approached through a walled and wrought iron railing entrance along a short drive to the front of the property. The reception rooms and bedrooms are bright with splendid views of the gardens and surrounding area including the scenic Rock of Cashel. The house has a south facing orientation and gives a true ambiance of peace. The outbuildings in the yard incorporate the original stone coach house as well as other store rooms and a garage. A wonderful opportunity not to be missed. SERVICES OFCH, mains water. Electricity supply, phone points, broadband, three phase power, new fully wired fire alarm HISTORY OF CASHEL TOWN Cashel is designated a heritage town attracting over 250,000 visitors per year, which boasts the world famous St Patrick's Rock of Cashel. This is the most visited heritage site in Ireland. The property is within walking distance of the Rock of Cashel which was the seat of the Kings of Munster and Medieval Bishops for 900 years. It was where Brian Boru was crowned King of Munster in 977 and he then became the High King of Ireland in 1003. HISTORY: Georgian home constructed in 1710. During 1830's and 1840's Hill House was home to Rev.Dean James McDonnell who fought a campaign against landlordism and injustice during the Irish Famine. This priest entertained Daniel O'Connell, The Liberator, who held one of his Monster Rallies in Cashel in 1846 with the objective of repealing the Act of Union. Between 1848 - 1883 Hill House was home to Honourable Martin Joseph French, who helped suppress the Young Irelander Rebellion of 1848 and the Fenian Rising in 1867. Hill House has evidence of some windows being boarded up. This dates back to the Window Tax in the mid 1700's when owners tried to reduce the number of windows and so reduce the tax! AMENITIES For golf enthusiasts Dundrum House Hotel and Golf Leisure Resort Course is 5 miles drive from Cashel. Those interested in hunting and racing could not be better placed with the local hunting pack being the famous Tipperary Hunt. Cashel will cater for all shopping requirements and also a range of primary and secondary schools. Rockwell college and The Ursuline Convent are located a within 10 miles. Thurles, Clonmel and Tipperary Racetracks are a short drive from Cashel. Larkspur Park Sports Complex has 5 tennis courts, sports hall and 18 Hole Pitch & Putt Course. Both Thurles and Limerick Junction train stations are 30 minute's drive from Cashel and are both main line trains to both Cork, Limerick and Dublin. LOCATION The property is located in the heart of Tipperary on the edge of the busy market town of Cashel, close to all the facilities that County Tipperary offers. Cashel is strategically located just off the M8 exit 7.,8 & 9 - Dublin 150 km/100 miles - Cork 93 km/58 miles - Waterford 70 km/44 miles - Limerick 61 km/38.1 miles - Cahir 18 km/11.25 miles - Thurles 22 km/14 miles
