There are many local hill-walking and hiking clubs and tours, sailing, sea-angling and fishing, kayaking, and surfing and water sports clubs. This is a county of unspoilt splendour where stark peaks and sweeping beaches bask in glorious sunshine, port-side restaurants serve innovative food, and small country pubs and isolated taverns provide a welcome drink and impromptu traditional music sessions. In recent years, the local food scene has been flourishing, and delicious fresh local seafood is never far away.\r\n\r\nDonegal's rugged interior, with its remote mountain passes and shimmering lakes, is only marginally outdone by the long and labyrinthine coastline with rugged cliffs and windswept peninsulas; a landscape so wild that Golden Eagles have successfully been reintroduced to the skies overlooking Glenveagh National Park (around 30 minutes' drive away). With its country roads, and a coastline that cuts and curves, the word 'escape' doesn't even begin to do it justice. The road runs out at the show-stopping Slieve League cliffs, dropping 600 metres into Donegal Bay; they are amongst the tallest in western Europe, an unforgettable hike. They are a highlight of the Wild Atlantic Way, the breathtaking road that runs all the way along the Western coast from Donegal to Cork in the south of Ireland.\r\n\r\nProudly independent, one-third of Donegal is official Gaeltacht - Irish speaking territory.\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\nPROPERTY DETAILS\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\nThis magnificent modern four-bedroom detached home sits on an elevated 1.32-acre private site with breath-taking open views of Mulroy Bay.
