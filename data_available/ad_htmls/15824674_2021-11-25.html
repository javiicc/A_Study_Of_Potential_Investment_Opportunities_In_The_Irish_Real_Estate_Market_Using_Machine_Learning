    Sherry FitzGerald takes great pleasure presenting this truly magnificent detached home, located within this picturesque, rural setting on the banks of the River Shannon. With magnificent views over the countryside, which extend all the way to the water and beyond, the property is set on c.14 acres of land, with paddocks, stabling and beautifully manicured gardens, making this is a unique opportunity for a discerning purchaser, especially those who require land for equestrian purposes.\n\nHaving been completely refurbished in 2015 the property combines elegantly proportioned rooms with, bright, modern interiors, which are complemented by a variety of characterful features and an extremely comfortable B2 energy rating. The quality of the renovation is evident throughout the home and every room is well presented, with a bright and warm feel from the very second you enter the large reception hall. The sitting room has a dual aspect with windows front and rear and a lovely feature fireplace with solid fuel stove providing the focal point for the room. The main reception area connects the modern kitchen/breakfast room to an open plan living/dining area, with access to two patios providing the perfect platform for entertaining at any time of year. There is a versatile bedroom 4/office on the ground floor with guest shower room ensuite. Upstairs are three fine size bedrooms, with great care and attention given to the principal and guest bedroom suites, both of which feature ensuite facilities, vaulted ceilings and exposed beams. The main bedroom is an incredible room with a bespoke floor to ceiling window and french doors that open to provide a stunning view towards the River Shannon and across the nearest paddocks. There is a large walk in wardrobe with hanging and shelving, which leads to the ensuite bathroom which has a freestanding clawfoot bath, adding to the character of the home.\n\nOutside the land is divided into a variety of paddocks with a track with separate roadside entrance leading to two fields of c.5 and c.6 acres. There is an arena and further paddocks just a short distance from the stable areas with several outdoor field shelters. There are further private gardens at the front rear and side, with a sweeping driveway leading from the road to the garage and parking area, which would cater for several vehicles. Finally, there is an historic building on the grounds, understood to be a former windmill which dates back to the early 18th century and now becomes a feature of the home.\n\nThe location is second to none and offers all the benefits of a tranquil and idyllic setting whilst still being just a 20km (approx.) commute of Limerick City Centre. Just a half kilometer walk of Ringmoylan Pier, well renowned for both fishing and swimming, the property is 2km (approx.) from the picturesque village of Pallaskenry which offers a variety of local shops and services, added to pubs, historic GAA club and Church. The property is within minutes drive of the N69 coast road, which connects south Limerick to Co. Kerry and the Wild Atlantic Way. The M7 and M20 motorway networks to Cork and Dublin and N18 to Shannon International Airport are also within easy reach.\n\n\nReception Hall Bright, welcoming entrance hall, with front door, recessed ceiling spotlights, tiled floor, staircase, large storage/cloaks cupboard.\n\nSitting Room Dual aspect reception room with recessed ceiling spotlights built in display cabinets, shelving and cupboards, feature fireplace with stone surround and solid fuel stove insert.\n\nKitchen/Breakfast Room With recessed ceiling spotlights, range of high gloss wall and floor level kitchen units, drawers and counter tops.
