    Exceptional Victorian residence built c.1860 which has been totally refurbished, extended and improved over the years enjoying magical views over Coliemore harbour, Dalkey Sound out towards Howth.\r\n\r\nSorrento is a most impressive Victorian residence built c.1860 by James Milo Burke who was also responsible for the build of nearby Victoria House and Springfield on Nerano Road. Throughout its history, it has played host to a wealth of fascinating occupants. Its unique L-shaped design has been improved and extended over the years. The most impactful are the recent improvements by the current owners who have carried out a thorough stylish refurbishment project managed and designed by Clare Hughes Interiors.\r\n\r\nA discreet entrance with electric gates leads the way through a tarmacadamed driveway which breaks out into luxurious loose pebbled driveway to reveal Sorrento in all its glory. A neatly positioned circular flower bed with granite cobble stones and clipped buxus hedging provides the perfect turning circle. The reassuringly luxurious scrunching of gravel announces your arrival. The eye is immediately drawn to the slate Mansard roof with well-appointed dormer windows and the strategically positioned orangery at the south east elevation providing a teasing glimpse of the magnificent views on offer.\r\n\r\nFully refurbished, restored and wonderfully maintained Sorrento provides an exceptional balance of generous accommodation and family friendly gardens. Level lawns provide an air of conventionality rendering it ideal for football kicking and fun-loving youngsters. The views from Sorrento are exceptional and whilst clich they are at times truly breath-taking. An abundance of sash windows, dormer windows, bay windows, a conservatory and a fully glazed wall to a sun terrace are positioned around the house to take full advantage of the stunning views over Coliemore Harbour, Dalkey Island and Sound, the Kish Lighhouse out as far as Howth.\r\n\r\nThrough the entrance porch is a welcoming reception hallway with wide oak stained floorboards, a Stanley wood burning stove and French doors opening out to the back garden. Stylish double-hung doors with gold hardware provide a quirky and practical solution for sharing light and keeping family pets or toddlers in one spot and lead to the kitchen. This is a truly special space and the owner has applied with more than just an eye for detail, a collection of truly impressive design touches which combine to create this impressive hub. A large centre island extending to 3.6m x 1.6m has an impressive worktop constructed using two carefully selected Grade A Calacatta slabs with a book matched joint and provides centre stage. Above it, a stunning light fitting, commission by the owners, of hand blown Venetian Murano glass from the Bisanzio Gallery complements the marble top to perfection and sets the tone for this truly impressive home. A bay window with window seat provides the perfect nook for informal dining, there is an AGA, a further window seat with antiqued mirror backdrop, larder cupboard, SubZero fridge and wine cooler. The kitchen opens up to a cosy orangery with twin sets of French doors to the back and front of the house, effortlessly connecting them allowing full enjoyment of maximum views and garden orientation. \r\n\r\nDownstairs there is a formal drawing room, dining room and a fully fitted Butler s kitchen.
