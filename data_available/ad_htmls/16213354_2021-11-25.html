    The Old Presbytery is an exceptional late Victorian detached home set on approximately 3.5 acres of mature grounds with views to the surrounding rugged dramatic landscape that is famed in Kerry whilst being within easy distance of the picturesque town of Kenmare. \n\nThis is a most attractive residence having been fully refurbished in 2015/2016. During the completion of the extensive renovation works, great care was taken to maintain its period features & charm; including the curved front staircase, remaining original floorboards, coving in the main reception rooms and high ceilings throughout enhancing the sense of space and light. During the course of the refurbishment, the roof was partially re-slated and completely re-felted, all windows were replaced, the front fa\xc3\xa9\xc2\xa7ade was re-rendered with a lime render, the boiler was replaced and all the radiators were upgraded to modern column radiators. \n\nInternally, The Old Presbytery has a modern contemporary aesthetic, like all good interiors, it feels effortless. Measuring approximately 233 sq m (2,500 sq ft), it is beautifully presented throughout. On entering, you are immediately struck by the light, which flows through the large sash windows, and the feeling of comfort in this family home. \n\nOff the main entrance you have the two principal reception rooms with the kitchen/dining room accessed by the hall or through the family room. The ground floor accommodation is finished with a guest wc and utility. Serviced by two staircases, the first floor is not interconnected, with two bedrooms and a bathroom located to the front and the third & fourth bedroom both with ensuite shower rooms located to the rear and accessed via the back stairs. \n\nOriginally built in 1897, the residence was in ecclesiastical use for approx. 40 years before the parishes of Bonane & Glengarriff merged. It was then taken over by the Irish Youth Hostel Association in the 1960's. Eventually being converted into a private home in the 1980's.\n\nThe grounds surrounding the house are mainly laid out in lawn with mature shrubs and flowering plants, there is also a most enchanting wooded area to the rear. \n\nThe property also has the benefit of a large detached outbuilding to the rear measuring circa 48 sq m which has electricity and is plumbed. \n\nSituated just over 10km from Kenmare town, a small tranquil country town, famous for its restaurants, pubs, cafes & independent artisan shops set amidst breath-taking scenery in one of the most unspoilt natural environments in Europe. Established as Ireland's first 'planned' town back in 1670, the historical, cultural and environmental significance of Kenmare was recognised when it was designated a 'Heritage Town'. \n\nAnyone interested in outdoor pursuits are well catered for in this part of South West Ireland with hill walking, fishing, golfing, cycling or multiple water-based activities.\n\nThe Old Presbytery is a sublime property, nestled in the Sheen valley, with views of the Caha mountains within the majestic kingdom of Kerry, and needs to be viewed to be appreciated.\n\nAccommodation \nWarm understated colour palette from Farrow and Ball with bespoke woodwork - skirting board and architrave throughout.\n\nGround Floor\n\nEntrance Porch: 1.9m x 3.0m.
