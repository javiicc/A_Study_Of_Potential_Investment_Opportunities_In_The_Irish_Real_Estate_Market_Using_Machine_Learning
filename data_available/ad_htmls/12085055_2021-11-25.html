    Arundel is a grand Victorian home located on the venerable Burrow Road, close to Cush Point in wonderful Sutton. The endearing facade envelops the splendid interior where historic charm and extraordinary craftsmanship reveal a very special and unique home. Panoramic views over Sutton Golf Course and the locally favoured Burrow Beach form the perfect backdrop for this period "beach-home" which will cast its spell over all who visit. \r\n\r\nDating from c.1890 the interior provides a spectacular reception hall ordained by original timber joinery and ornate floor tiles. Three gracious, generously proportioned reception rooms lead off this space. Each room offers an individual ambience and period features from splendid original mantlepieces, wide bay windows and natural timber panels. \r\n\r\nA cosy "snug" bar is an authentic entertaining space complete with timber pine panels, stools and antique memorabilia that creates a marvellous atmosphere in this space. The handcrafted kitchen breakfast room is a welcoming comfortable family area while both drawing and dining rooms enjoy tall ceilings and plenty of natural light. \r\n\r\nThe informal "garden" living room has timber panelled ceilings and walls salvaged from old pine doors and here French sliding doors open into the rear garden.\r\n\r\nUpstairs off the landing are five spacious double bedrooms, one en-suite bathroom and a family bathroom. The second bedroom leads to a terrace with views to Ireland's Eye and Lambay Island.\r\n\r\nThe rear garden borders the natural links terrain of the adjacent golf course, a fitting backdrop to Arundel. The garden is laid out in extensive flower beds and lawn, a tranquil gazebo, a sunken sun trap patio and a raised pond. The garden is immaculate and sheltered providing a private outside space which is ideal for taking in the maritime surrounds of this remarkable home. \r\n\r\nThis location is a sporting enthusiasts dream with the prestigious Sutton golf club bordering the property and nearby Sutton tennis club, rugby club and dingy club. Nature lovers will delight in the sandy Burrow beach just three minutes walk away and appreciate the local designated nature conservation area. Close by Sutton cross and Howth village offer a wide selection of eateries from cafes to fine dining and casual local stores.
