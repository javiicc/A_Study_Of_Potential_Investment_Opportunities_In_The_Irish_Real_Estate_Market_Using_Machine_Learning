    For sale subject to planning \r\n\r\nThis elevated 0.94 acre building plot is positioned just 5 mins from both Milford and Ramelton. Enjoying stunning views over lake the site is being sold subject to planning. It should be noted that the council will only permit planning to those who are from this area and who are building as a principle family home.\r\n\r\nLocal Area Guide Video: www.franklins.ie/area-guides/fanad \r\n\r\nStronger Rural Areas\r\nIt is a policy of the council that they consider proposals from prospective applicants in need of housing within an area defined as Stronger Rural Area, , where the applicant can demonstrate that they comply with one or more of the following;\r\n\r\n\xe2\x82\xac\xc2\xa2 Persons whose primary employment is in a rural based activity with a demonstrated genuine need to live in the locality of that employment base e.g agriculture, forestry\r\n\xe2\x82\xac\xc2\xa2 Persons with a vital link to the rural area by reason of having lived in this community for a substantial period of their lives (7 years minimum), or by the existence in the rural area of long established ties (7 years minimum) with immediate family members, or by reason of providing care to a person who is an existing resident (7 years minimum)\r\n\xe2\x82\xac\xc2\xa2 Persons who for exceptional health circumstances can demonstrate a genuine need to reside in a particular rural location\r\n\r\nPlease note; the policy shall not apply where an individual has already had the benefit of a permission for a dwelling on another site, unless exceptional circumstances can be demonstrated.\r\nNew holiday home developments will not be permitted in these areas.\r\n\r\nResidential building plot\r\nFor sale subject to planning\r\n0.94 acres\r\nStunning views over lake\r\nJust 5 mins from Ramelton & Milford\r\nZoned Stronger rural area\r\n\r\nFrom Ramelton proceed in the direction of Milford and turn right at the sharp bend signposted Ray.\r\n\r\nProceed along this road for approximately 750 and take the first right turn after the lake. The site will then be found after 200 m on the left hand side just after a property with entrance pillars and gates\r\n\r\nThe Mall on the River Lennon offers a limited range of shops including a mini supermarket, chemist, etc. whilst c.4 miles in the direction of Milford there is a well patronised Lidl Supermarket.\r\n\r\nThe name Ramelton derives from the Irish name R\xc3\xa9\xc2\xa1th Mealtain which means The Fort of Mealtain. It is a Bord Failte designated Heritage Town on the banks of the River Lennon. The old warehouses at the end of The Mall are amongst the most fascinating buildings in Donegal. They formed the backdrop for the highly acclaimed BBC Television Series \xe2\x82\xacThe Hanging Gale\xe2\x82\xacTM\r\n\r\nThe town now has its own park and tennis club plus a good range of local shops. The famous Bridge Bar\xe2\x82\xacTMis a renowned music venue and has its own restaurant. Easy access is afforded to the coastal areas of Rathmullan, Portsalon and Dunfanaghy (each around 15 minutes distant). Letterkenny, the county principal town is just 12 -15 minutes away. Local schools include Drumman N.S., St. Marys N.S., Ayr Hill N.S. and Scoil Naomh Cholmcille.
