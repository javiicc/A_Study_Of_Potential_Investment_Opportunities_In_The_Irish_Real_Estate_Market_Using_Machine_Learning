Hidden from the road, you enter through a stone wall entrance onto a gravel drive which is fenced and hedged on either side leading up to the courtyard. The enclosed south westerly courtyard is entered through gates and provides access to the entrance hall of the property. The courtyard is paved with cobblelock, dotted with mature flowering plants and shrubs including a mature Japanese Maple. The gravel driveway continues around the side of the property providing access to the garage 2.31 x 2.42. The property also boasts a greenhouse, two stables, stable 1 3.36 x 2.42 and stable 2 5.90 x 2.47 with a storage area 1.21 x 5.66. A patio can be found to the south east side of the property and easily accessed via a sliding door to the family room and the kitchen cum breakfast room. The remainder of the property is laid out in two paddocks and the lawn at the rear is bounded by a wooded area which in turn slopes down to a flat area extending to the bank of the river Boyne.\r\n\r\n\r\nLOCATION\r\n\r\nThe property enjoys the best of both worlds located approximately 4km from Navan Town Centre, convenient to Johnstown Village, adjoining the River Boyne and a wide variety of amenities. For the necessary services there are several pre-, primary and secondary schools together with healthcare facilities including Our Lady's Hospital. A complete spectrum of retailers both local and national are on offer and there is a host of recreational choices; a wide choice of sporting facilities, 6 screen Cineplex, restaurants, pubs, Solstice Arts Centre and much more.\r\n\r\nFor those with an interest in history and/or walking for leisure you are spoiled for choice from this address;- the Rivers Boyne and Blackwater, Seven arch railway viaduct over river constructed circa1849 of rock faced rusticated limestone, the ruins of 'Athlumney Castle' a part Norman/part Tudor castle, old Athlumney Church with graveyard and your nearest and perhaps most interesting walking amenity - 'The Ramparts', a local beauty spot affording walks along the old Boyne Canal. The canal was built in 1759 ceased commercial use and now provides a walk which begins where the Blackwater & Boyne Rivers meet and continues for approximately 8km as far as Stackallen with some stunning scenic views, interesting architecture and a wide variety of wildlife to watch. With such picturesque surroundings the property has a true feel of country living within easy reach of Navan town.\r\n\r\nThe property is excellently located on the southern side of town (Dublin side), just off the R147 (former N3), a short distance from the M3 interchange. There is easy access to Dublin City via the R147 and M3. For those using public transport, CIE provide an excellent commuter service between Navan & Dublin City Centre. The nearest 109bus stop is less than a 10 minute walk from the property ('Navan, Kilcarn Bridge') & the 109X bus stop being a 20 minute walk ('Navan, Opp Ardboyne Hotel') with buses to Dublin regularly starting from 5.43am - 10.40m daily. The NX runs every 20 minutes daily from 5.49am - 12.41pm with the stop located only 15 minute walk from the property ('Navan, Opp IDA Ireland')\r\n \r\n\r\nDIRECTIONS: EIRCODE: C15 V1W0\r\n\r\nFrom Dublin take the M3 to Navan, exiting at Junction 8 (20 to 25 minutes from M50). Travel to the roundabout, take the 2nd right hand turn (at Willows Restaurant). The property is 500m up the road on the left hand side\r\n\r\n\r\nPlease Note\r\n\r\nPlease note we have not tested any apparatus, fixtures, fittings, or services.
