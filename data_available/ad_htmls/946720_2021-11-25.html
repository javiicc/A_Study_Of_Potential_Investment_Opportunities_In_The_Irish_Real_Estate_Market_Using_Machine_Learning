      ** Final Home Remaining **\r\n\r\nMaydenhayes near Mornington in County Meath is an established development situated where the River Boyne meets the Irish Sea and is only minutes from the M1 northern corridor, the M50 and the national motorway network Thanks to major road developments in recent years this charming area, is home to all kinds of new amenities including shops, schools, churches and sport and leisure facilities. \r\n\r\nMaydenhayes is within easy reach of all points North, South and Central. Only 40 minutes from Dublin City Centre, 30 minutes from Swords and Dublin Airport, Maydenhayes is also readily accessible to the new shopping centres at Southgate, Scotch Hall and Drogheda itself. As well as being convenient to the many important business and industrial parks located along this great thoroughfare all the way north to Belfast and south as far as Wexford.\r\n\r\nMaydenhayes is only a 5 mins drive to the seaside town of Bettystown and also just 10 minutes' drive to the busy market town of Drogheda which offers shops and services for all. Packed brim-full of great shops, fine restaurants and nice bars, Drogheda is also well served by doctors, dentists and a full range of professional services.\r\n\r\nFor further information or to register your details, please contact Bridie Cafferkey on 086 145 0736 or visit www.maydenhayes.ie

      Location

      For those who work in the capital regular bus and rail services connect Drogheda with Dublin and excellent roads make short work of commuting. The M1 to Belfast is also easily accessed.\r\n\r\nAdd to that Drogheda\xe2\x80\x99s proud history and colourful heritage, best symbolised perhaps by the housing of the head of Blessed Oliver Plunkett in the town centre church, and you have a potent mix of past and present glories.\r\n\r\nMaydenhayes is at the heart at the heart of the Boyne Valley region, perhaps the most archaeologically significant area in the country, with sites such as Newgrange, Dowth and Mellifont Abbey all close by.\r\n\r\nFull of life, there are tons of interesting outlets when it comes to sports, recreation and leisure.
