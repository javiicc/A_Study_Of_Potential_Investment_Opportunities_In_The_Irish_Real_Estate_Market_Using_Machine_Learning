    Number 5 Knocknacree Park is an A rated five bedroom family home of approximately 235sq.m (2,530sq.ft), presented in turnkey condition throughout. It occupies an elevated position in this highly regarded cul-de-sac boasting breathtaking unrivalled panoramic views across Dublin Bay to Howth from the kitchen/living/terrace and bedrooms upstairs. \n\nThe property was extended and renovated in 2017 under the guidance of Kavanagh Ryan Architects and has all the benefits of a new build including: solar panels, an air to water heat pump system, an electric car charging point, external insulation, and double-glazed Munster Joinery Alu-clad windows. Internally the accommodation comprises at hall level of a lobby with cloaks hanging area, opening through to a double height entrance hallway, off this there is a study/home office, a double bedroom, shower room, a cosy snug sitting room. To the rear there is a superb semi open plan kitchen/dining/family room with panoramic views out to Howth, a large slider opens out onto the terrace which is the perfect spot to take in the view. Stairs lead down from the kitchen to the lower garden level which can be self-contained for guests and off the kitchen there is a large utility/pantry with access out to the side passageway. At first floor level there are three well proportioned bedrooms, each offering wonderful views, the master bedroom is en-suite, there are views from the landing up to Dalkey Quarry. There is a good amount of eaves storage space accessible from landing, and a large family bathroom completes the accommodation at this level. At garden level there is a kitchen/dining/sitting room and a fifth bedroom with en-suite, French doors open from here out to the rear garden. This space has the added benefit of being self-contained for guests/teenagers or an au-pair. \n\nTo the front of Number 5 there are vehicular gates opening into a cobble lock driveway providing off street parking for two cars, there is an electric charge point and the garden is surrounded with high hedging, is very private and faces due south. There is a garden shed, space for wheelie bins, side access and granite steps leading down to the front door. Off the living accommodation at hall level there is a low maintenance balocny with artificial lawn and a glazed balustrade, from here there are stunning views out to Howth. The rear garden is very nicely landscaped with a limestone patio and measures approximately 20m in length, there is a lawn with bedded borders and pathway leading to the end where there is a suntrap seating area with artificial lawn\n\nThe position of this quality home will be of major interest to those trying to get a foothold on the highly regarded much sought after Dalkey location. This coastal town offers a wide range of local and specialist shops, cafes, delicatessens, excellent pubs and some of the finest restaurants in South County Dublin. The surrounding area is well served by an excellent selection of primary and secondary schools including Loreto Abbey Dalkey, Harold Boys, Castle Park, Holy Child, Rathdown and the Harold National School. Dalkey DART Station is literally around the corner and this services the city centre as well as many boys\xe2\x80\x99 schools with regular ease. Dalkey also caters for many recreational and leisure facilities with Cuala GAA and Dalkey United situated close by as well as golf, tennis and hockey clubs. There are also many fitness centres and the tranquil scenic walks over Killiney and Dalkey hills and along the seafront are particularly popular.
