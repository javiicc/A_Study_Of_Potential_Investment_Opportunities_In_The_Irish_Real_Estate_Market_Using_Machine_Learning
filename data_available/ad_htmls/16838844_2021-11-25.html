    Description

    • Sale Type: For Sale by Private Treaty
    A wonderful late 19th Century country retreat lovingly restored and extended, with the use of many pleasing architectural features which enhance this superb home and maximise a truly unique setting brimming with tranquillity amidst groves of trees, shrubs, pathways and pastures extending to circa 3 acres and close to the north shore of Lough Key \n\nViewing Highly Recommended.\nEntrance Porch 1.40m x 1.25m. with side window, quarry tiles, alarm & radiator.\n\nSittingroom 4.66m x 3.71m. Carpet, Henley Eco Solid Fuel Stove on slate hearth, oak curtain pole & drapes, recessed downlighters, radiator, Bay window feature.\n\nLiving/Dining 4.74m x 4.70m. Quarry floor tiling, Rayburn cast iron, solid fuel range (linked to water heating and radiators) with tiled back & oak manel, radiator, recessed downlighters, large window with wood slat blinds.\n\nKitchen/Dining 7.63m x 3.69m. A superb, naturally lit, double height space. Fitted Shaker kitchen with Zanussi ceramic hob, stainless steel extraction hood, Zanussi fridge freezer, integrated dishwasher, Bosch washer. Bronze task track lighting, French doors with glazed side panels out to garden. \nWonderful sloped ceiling feature with 6 large Velux roof lights.\n\nBedroom 1/ Library/Lounge 4.44m x 3.75m. T&G wood flooring, Large windows with low sills taking advantage of garden aspect, radiator, wooden curtain pole & linen drapes, library wall shelf, French doors and glazed side panels out to garden.\n\nSide Hallway 2.58m x 1.16m. Tiled floor, radiator, access to roof space.\n\nWet Room Slate & mosaic tiling, Triton T80 shower, bidet, wc & whb, radiator, heated towel rail, shaver light.\n\nRear Hallway 2.58m x 1.18m. Tiled floor, hardwood door with glazed panel.\n\nOak stairs, carpeted to first floor.\n\nBedroom 2 4.74m x 2.90m. T&G wood flooring, vaulted ceiling, wall sconces, wall shelf, curtain pole & drapes, radiator, recessed wardrobe with curtain pole & tweed drape.\n\nLanding Area 5.57m2. Carpeted, airing cupboard/solar panel controls, Joule hot water cylinder, access to roof.\n\nBedroom 3 3.20m x 1.69m. Carpeted, vaulted ceiling.\n\nBedroom 4 2.83m x 2.65m. Carpeted, vaulted ceiling, curtain pole & drapes, radiator.\n\nBathroom 2.46m x 1.64m. Painted wood flooring, panelled bath with Triton Shower over, chrome fittings, wall panelling, wall mirror, radiator, wc & whb.\n\nSelf Contained Apartment Separate Entrance, Sitting/Dining Area, Galley Kitchen, Shower-room/wc, Mezzanine in roof apex with a double bed. \nSuitable for family or friends for overnights or short term tenancies or use as a separate self-contained office.\n\nGENERAL Water coming into the main house is filtered (to prevent limescale)and there is a further filter in the kitchen for drinking water.\nAttic is floored and provides a substantial storage area.\nOFCH\nSolid Fuel Stove\nHybrid Solar Panel System\nAntique bronze light switches & sockets\nLovely traditional wood joinery with sheeted internal doors & iron mongery\nT&G wood stained flooring\n\nEXTERNAL Heating is provided by an oil boiler (external) with radiators in all rooms supported by solid fuel stoves in 3 rooms and a storage heater in the studio.\n\n\nThere are 3 sheds on the grounds with one containing chest freezers and a tumble dryer and controls for the water treatment system and an adjoining shed for cutting and storing wood. A third shed at the top of the garden is wired and is currently used for writing and relaxation.\n\n\nThe house is fitted with an alarm system which can be remotely controlled as can the oil boiler.\nSecurity cameras cover the front and rear of the house and can be remotely viewed and controlled.\nThere is parking for at least 4 cars at the main entrance and a works entrance below the house can provide access for larger vehicles.\n\n\nThe house is set on 3 acres with flowers and shrubs to the front and raised vegetable beds and fruit trees and bushes to the rear. Steps lead up under a canopy of red dogwood shrubs to a double seat built into a section of the rear wall of the garden. Close to the entrance area is a small pond attracting wildlife and a walkway to fields combining elements of rewilding and recently planted trees. Large trees surround the house and all close to the house have recently undergone surgery to ensure safety of the house. \nThe range of trees on site include:\nBeech - Ash - Chesnut - Alder - Sycamore - Hawthorn - Hazel - Willow - Cherry - Cedar - Pines - Himalayan Birch, Norwegian Maple, Swamp Cypress, and a recently planted Sequoia. Holm (evergreen) oak and a variety of other oaks have also been planted along with some Persian Ironwood. Privately owned forests nearby have recently been harvested and replanted with a mixture of oak and spruce. An area of this behind the rear garden is being left to rewild. Deer and badger are the most common visitors with an occasional red squirrel, fox and pine-marten.\n\n\nDIRECTIONS:\nF52 AT26
