    Grianblah 26 Palmerston Park is a stunning architect designed contemporary two-storey family home set on what is undoubtedly one of the finest sites in Dublin, a private parkland 0-5 acre approx. site overlooking Palmerston Park. \r\nIt strikes an unparalleled balance of a rural ambiance in an urban setting. \r\n\r\nThe previous house on the site, while dating from the mid twentieth century, carried some features of the Arts and Crafts design movement popular at the beginning of the twentieth century. The design of the existing house, which shares part of the footprint of the previous, continues that design theme, referencing the works of Charles Voysey and Edwin Lutyens, two prominent Arts and Crafts architects who specialised in the design of large country houses mostly in England. \r\n\r\nArts and Crafts houses frequently include dominant steeply sloping roof planes, asymmetrical front gables, projecting eyebrow bands over windows and oversized chimneys with feature curved cappings. The house includes several of these design devices, echoing, in particular, the Voysey Orchard House in Chorleywood, Hertfordshire.\r\n\r\nDesigned by renowned architects McCrossan O Rourke Manning, Grianblah was built in 2007 to exacting standards. \r\nThe brief to the architects was to take maximum advantage of the site. No expense was spared to make this a most elegant yet very practical and comfortable family home with an impressive B1 energy rating. \r\nSet behind original granite walls the exceptional privacy of the site and outlook onto Palmerston Park create the feeling of being in the countryside. The location is superb close all the local amenities of Ranelagh and Rathmines and just minutes stroll to the Luas stops at Milltown and Beechwood and Cowper. \r\n\r\nSet over two levels this is a versatile easily managed home with elegant reception rooms and generous bedroom accommodation.\r\nA reception hallway with impressive oak Scarlet O Hara staircase has the wow factor as has the formal reception room at first floor with a large terrace perfect for relaxing and enjoying the views over the park. Bedrooms are situated on both ground and first floor. The large kitchen /dining room /family room is at the heart of the house with dual aspects again to maximize the view. Also on the ground floor is the home office and conservatory / garden room overlooking the magnificent rear garden. Another wing houses a sitting room, a bedroom and a versatile games room/home gym. This wing could be easily be separated to provide privacy for a granny flat / separate suite. \r\n\r\nThe gardens, south facing to the rear, extending to 0-5 acre approx. are a real feature, landscaped with a large terrace and patio a sweeping lawn south framed by mature hedging and trees. A large brick garage provides exceptional storage. \r\n\r\nThe location is spectacular, an area in which people generally stay long-term so close to all the local amenities of Dublin 6 including excellent schools, high quality shopping in Ranelagh & Rathmines with specialist food shops on Dunville avenue and Upper Rathmines road and a host of cafes, bars and restaurants. Palmerston Park is a lovely local amenity and the sports facilities of Milltown Golf club, David Lloyd Fitness, and Brookfield Tennis club to name just a few are within very easy reach.\r\n\r\nThe privacy, peace and tranquillity of the surroundings, combined with the accessible location make Grianblah a real draw.\r\n\r\n\r\nReception Hall Reception hall feature central staircase, Cr me Marfil floor, recessed lights, coving, double height ceiling (in part).\r\n\r\nw.c. 2.53m x 2.51m.
