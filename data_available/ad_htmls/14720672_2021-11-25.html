    Traditional 2 bed country cottage in a coastal setting\r\n\r\nDescription \r\nKate' s Country Cottage is a 200-year-old picture-perfect home set amidst the beautiful, rugged landscape of Liscannor in County Clare. \r\n\r\nThe cottage is accessed off a rural country road through two stone piers leading to a parking area at the side of the house. The cottage is located along the Wild Atlantic Way and just 500m from the R478 which connects the Cliffs of Moher to Lahinch. \r\n\r\nKate' s Country Cottage has been renovated to a very high standard yet maintains many traditional features including the stunning Moher and Liscannor flagstone floors and of course, the stone interior. The cottage features a stove and an open fire which adds to the atmosphere within the property. \r\n\r\nUpon entering the cottage, you are greeted with a bright spacious kitchen. The kitchen leads to a light filled living room with comfortable accommodation. \r\n\r\nThe bedroom accommodation is laid out at either end of the house comprising two double bedrooms with one en-suite. The cottage also includes a main bathroom with a standalone shower.\r\n\r\nOutbuildings \r\nA key feature to the property is the extensive range of traditional outbuildings, which have been restored and maintained by the present owners. Accessed via a gate to the side of the cottage the outbuildings comprise a large Dutch barn with concrete block walls and two double doors. There are three smaller stores located here which are of traditional construction with stone walls and timber doors. These buildings are multi-functional and could be converted to further accommodation (bedroom, office etc) subject to the necessary planning permissions. \r\n\r\nGrounds\r\nSituated to the rear of the cottage is a beautiful, raised garden which is predominantly laid to lawn and is bordered by a charming traditional stone wall. The raised garden includes glorious sea views. To the front of the property is a sun trap patio area with a quaint stone bench comprising a magnificent outlook over the rolling countryside. \r\n\r\nGeneral Remarks\r\n\r\nVIEWING\r\nStrictly by appointment by Savills Country Agency.\r\n\r\nFixtures & Fittings\r\nAll fixtures and fittings are excluded from the sale including garden statuary, light fittings, and other removable fittings, although some items may be available by separate negotiation.\r\n\r\nSERVICES\r\nOiled-fired central heating, fibre optic broadband, mains water and private drainage. \r\n\r\nPlease be advised that the selling agents have not checked the services and any purchaser should satisfy themselves with the availability and adequacy of all services.\r\n\r\nEntry & Possession\r\nEntry is by agreement with vacant possession. \r\n\r\nOffers\r\nOffers may be submitted to the selling agents, Savills, 33 Molesworth Street, Dublin 2. \r\n\r\n\r\nBest Offers Date\r\nA date for best offers may be fixed and prospective purchasers are asked to register their interest with the selling agents following inspection. The sellers reserve the right to exchange a Contract for the sale of any part of the subjects of sale ahead of a notified closing date and will not be obliged to accept the highest or any offer.\r\n\r\nFinancial Guarantee\r\nAll offers (regardless of the country of residence of the offering party) must be accompanied by a guarantee or suitable form of reference from a bank, which gives the sellers satisfaction that the purchaser has access to the funds required to complete the purchase at the offered price.\r\n\r\nGenerally\r\nShould there be any discrepancy between these, the General Remarks and Information, Stipulations and the Contract of Sale, the latter shall prevail.\r\n\r\nCounty Clare is renowned for its dramatic and changing landscape, from the world-renowned Cliffs of Moher (2.5 km) to the unique limestone landscape of the Burren (14.4 km) with its extraordinary flora and fauna as well as ancient monuments. \r\n\r\nKate' s Country Cottage is located along the Wild Atlantic Way, just 2.5 kilometres from the spectacular Cliffs of Moher. One of Ireland' s most famous tourist attractions, The Cliffs of Moher has featured in classic movies like The Princess Bride and Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince. At 700 feet (213 metres) the Cliffs of Moher stretch south for nearly eight kilometres between Doolin and Hags head, with spectacular, breath-taking views. \r\n\r\nThe vibrant seaside town of Lahinch, which is nestled in a magnificent coastal position at the head of Liscannor Bay is just 8 kilometres distant. Lahinch offers a number of great restaurants, pubs, cafes, shops and hotels. A more comprehensive range of facilities and services can be found at Ennistymon (8 km), a pretty market town famous for its Victorian shop fronts, courthouse gallery, shops and restaurants. \r\n\r\nHome to a 2 km long beach with golden sands, Lahinch is renowned as one of the top holiday resorts in Ireland. This coastal town attracts surfing and canoeing enthusiasts from all over the world and has earned a reputation as a top ' surf centre' with multiple surf schools in the area. Lahinch has a wealth of activities for children and lovers of water sports including, sailing, swimming, kite surfing, diving and fresh water fishing.\r\n\r\nThe keen golfer is well catered for with superb golf courses nearby including the championship links course at Lahinch (7 km). Lahinch Golf Club is a magnificent links golf course worthy of it' s ranking in the World' s Top 50 and host to the 2019 Dubai Duty Free Irish Open. Woodstock Golf Club (38 km) Spanish Point Golf Club (22 km) and Trump International Golf Links at Doonbeg (36 km) are also close by. \r\n\r\nA variety of local schooling is available in Lahinch and Ennistymon. A wider range of schooling can be found in nearby Ennis.\r\n\r\n\r\nDIRECTIONS:\r\nEircode\r\nV95 XN2 E.
