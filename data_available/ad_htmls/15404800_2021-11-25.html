    It's rare that a jewel such as 8 Martello Terrace property comes to the market. This three storey property is part of the most recognisable and iconic terrace on Bray seafront. They stand proudly facing Bray Head and backing onto Bray Harbour. This is a really great location. A terrace of eight listed buildings that define the Northern end of Bray seafront.\r\n\r\nPJ O'Dwyer's are delighted to present this beautiful house to the market which has many original features of its era but is also a wonderful family home that sits on the northern end of Bray Promenade. This house forms part of one of Bray's earliest, best preserved, and most distinctive seafront terraces. The terrace has some historical value due to James Joyce living in No 1 between 1887 and 1891. No 8 is an end-of-terrace, three-storey house, one of a largely uniform row of eight houses built in 1860. \r\n\r\n A veranda and first floor Regency style balcony, encased by the decorative geometric Chinoiserie railings, spans the front of these beautiful homes. \r\n\r\nThe entrance, which is to the west elevation, consists of a beautifully painted bespoke hardwood timber door with a small hood supported on decorative brackets, and a segmental-headed fanlight above. The windows are new flat-headed and filled with two over two hardwood timber sash frames. A glazed door to the first floor leads onto the balcony with painted stone sills. The property faces onto Bray's grass-covered esplanade but is separated from it by a small private space now mainly used for car-parking.\r\n\r\nTo sit on the balcony with views towards Bray Head and along the coast towards Greystones is a true delight. The view along the promenade takes in Bray's seafront and a short walk around the corner has you on the edge of Bray Harbour and its walks up the back beach.\r\n\r\nThere are beautiful sea views to the front and the back with a clear view of the Cross on the top of Bray Head. It's a real pleasure to sit on the balcony with sea breezes and then retreat to the back garden where you are surrounded by the old original stone walls that provides privacy and sun all day.\r\nThis property has been well maintained and the sash windows throughout were double glazed in 2017. The bathroom & kitchen were updated in 2017 also.\r\n \r\nNew rendering & external down pipes, fascia & soffit were fitted to Irish Heritage standards in 2017/2018.\r\n\r\nThis house has seen many events since 1860 but has been a happy family home for the last forty years. It offers the opportunity to live in a spacious house with the benefits of Bray on your doorstep.\r\nBray has numerous restaurants, bars and shops and the Dart allows easy access to the city and the southern suburbs.
