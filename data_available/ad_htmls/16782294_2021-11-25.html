It has a subtle open staircase that leads to a cute loft space.\r\n\r\nThe second bedroom benefits from incredible natural light and boasts similar heritage features as well as a unique mezzanine space to maximise the functionality of this room.\r\n\r\nThe main reception room leads into the property spacious kitchen with traditional pitch pine fitted units, another wonderful timber-panelled vaulted ceiling and access onto a charming paved/patio area to the side of the house.\r\n\r\nThe property bathroom (featuring an electric shower) is in a hallway just outside the kitchen. The hall leads into a very generous utility space that incorporates a second bathroom featuring a toilet and sink.\r\n\r\nThe cottage at Carrowkilleen boasts a high standard of decorative finish throughout with fresh paintwork in a neutral mix of cool and earthy tones and unique period features like exposed timber lintels, traditional latched doors and more.\r\n\r\nIt also features a very generous detached garage and a pretty, enclosed garden space with ample parking.\r\n\r\nDIRECTIONS\r\nCarrowkilleen is located on a small, quiet country road just off the L1606 route that skirts along the shore of Lough Carra and links the village of Carnacon to Hollymount.\r\n\r\nThe latter is the nearest town to this property and marks the midway point between the bustling commercial centres of Ballinrobe (capital of Mayo Lake District) and Claremorris.\r\n\r\nMayo county town of Castlebar is a half hour commute away while Galway city can be reached in just under one hour drive as can Ireland West Airport Knock.\r\n\r\nVIEWING\r\nThis cottage is likely to appeal to a wide variety of purchasers from first-time buyers to remote workers, downsizers, and holiday home investors.\r\n\r\nEarly viewing is therefore highly recommended.\r\n\r\nProperty Partners Emma Gill strives to be ultra-flexible around property viewings.\r\nFor further information call (087) 7999955 or (094) 9542819/(091) 884000.
