    Quillsen is delighted to present to the market this very elegant and deceptively spacious period home that boasts amongst its many appealing features, a delicate fusion of contemporary design and classic elegance. \nWonderfully located within a leisurely stroll of the city centre, and immediately adjacent to all that Ranelagh village has to offer, Mountpleasant Square is possibly one of the nicest Georgian Squares in Dublin. No 26 is very attractively positioned on the square, and nestled with that all important sunny westerly aspect to the rear, it offers the discerning purchaser the convenience of city living amidst the privacy and serenity of a country home setting.\nExtending to approximately 200 sqm / 2,153 sqft, the accommodation comprises, at entrance level, a spacious entrance hall, 2 exceptionally bright interconnecting reception rooms and a cloakroom. At garden level, a comfortable living room area to the front opens through to a kitchen cum breakfast / dining room area at the rear. The breakfast/ dining room area opens out to a sunny west facing walled garden and overall, the garden level provides the ideal setting for entertaining and dining Al Fresco beneath the setting evening sun.\nThe first floor comprises 2 excellent double bedrooms, a single bedroom and a family bathroom. The master bedroom has spectacular views to the dome of Rathmines Church. The attic floor is converted to provide a spacious and very flexible room that is currently used as a bedroom. A spacious shower room completes the attic floor accommodation. \nThe convenience of the location speaks for itself. Mountpleasant Square is superbly located in the heart of Ranelagh and within strolling distance of the city centre, Rathmines and Donnybrook. It quite literally has everything on its doorstep including the abundance of bijoux shops, trendy bars and restaurants in Ranelagh, the beautiful fusion of open greenery, mature trees and children\xe2\x82\xacTM play facilities at Ranelagh Gardens Park, the Mount Pleasant Lawn Tennis Club on the centre green, the Fitzwilliam Tennis Club and excellent cinema options nearby at Rathmines.\nIn the other direction, Baggot Street, St Stephen's Green, Grafton Street, Temple Bar, the National Concert Hall, Iveagh Gardens, Trinity College and the Royal College of Surgeons are all within strolling distance. The Docklands together with the Bord Gais Theatre and the European headquarters of Google and Facebook is within walking distance and the hub of Dublin's financial and banking industry at the IFSC, together with the National College of Ireland, the National Convention Centre and the 3 Arena are also all within easy reach.\nTo complete the picture, and for the growing family, there is a choice of renowned Junior and Senior schools close by including St Mary's, Sandford National, Muckross and Gonzaga to mention but a few.
