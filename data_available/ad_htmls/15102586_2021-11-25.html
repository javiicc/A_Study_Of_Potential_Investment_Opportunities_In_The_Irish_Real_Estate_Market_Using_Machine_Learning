    A magnificent late-Victorian detached villa in the leafy heart of Dublin 6. Hatherton was built in 1860.Throughout its history, it has played host to a wealth of fascinating occupants, and witnessed a changing Ireland, through the emergence of an independent Republic and the ebb and flow of the country s fortunes. Now beautifully restored, Hatherton is a private oasis ready to welcome the next generation. \n\nOn just under an acre of grounds, set back from the road and secluded by mature trees, Hatherton has six bedrooms over approx. 696 sq.m. (7,491 sq.ft.), and a separate two bedroomed garden mews. The house includes magnificent formal rooms, exceptionally comfortable family spaces, and a self-contained staff suite. \n\nYour own spacious, elegant retreat in the heart of leafy Dublin 6. \n\nDistance\n Rathmines 1.5km approx \n Rathgar 2.5km approx\n St Stephen s Green 3.5km approx\n Dublin Airport 19km approx\n\nFully refurbished, sensitively restored, and wonderfully maintained, Hatherton is a perfect example of how to celebrate the period charms of such a house, while providing ample space for relaxed family life. The meticulous nature of the restoration has emphasised the beauty of the building, while the exacting attention to detail of the owners, their architect and craftspeople is breath-taking. \n\nPedimented doors, Corinthian columns, ceiling coving and centre roses are grounded by solid wood, and polished limestone floors as the hallway leads to a quartet of exceptionally elegant reception rooms, where the formality of their grand aspect is offset by the pervasive and welcoming atmosphere of the house. From the dual aspect drawing room, dining room and sitting room, each with its own marble fireplace, to the joy of the bar, where you ll find the wonderful feel of a favourite club, this is a house where entertaining is a delight.\n\nTall windows give views to the gardens, or to the mature trees that screen the fa ade, while high ceilings let in all that wonderful light. At garden level, family living comes to the fore, with a large and bright kitchen. Here you have both an Aga and a separate top of the range oven and hob, and all the prep space and surfaces a chef could desire. Beyond this a family room and breakfast room each open to the gardens, while a separate entertainment room makes a very worthy and wonderful den.\n\nUpstairs, the four bedrooms are all large ensuite doubles, with gorgeous views of the tree tops. They have ample storage solutions and a deliciously serene feeling. There is also an office at the bedroom return, along with a toilet located beside it, convenient if another bedroom should ever be required. Although, this is unlikely, as there are two further double bedrooms at garden level, one including a bathroom and kitchen, which serves as ideal accommodation for an au pair, nanny or housekeeper or convenient accommodation for someone elderly, who might prefer to avoid the stairs and another generous double with ensuite currently used by one of the owners sons.\n\nShould a home office be required, there is no shortage of space for an entire suite of offices, a gym, games room, store, or indeed a swimming pool in the extensive 1,752 square feet provided by the contemporary mews accommodation currently laid out as a two bedroom apartment and a separate gym and store.\n\n\nHall Level\n\nHall: Hatherton entrance hall makes a statement from the moment you step through the leaded doors of the outer hall and into the magnificent main hall.\n\nA classical entrance to a gracious family home via limestone flagstones inset with slate followed by polished timber floorboards and surrounded by fantastic period features: traditional arches, Pedimented doors, Corinthian columns painted in faux marble by decorative artist Noel Donnellan, and pilasters, ornate ceiling cornicing, centre pieces and ceiling roses. Neat flower beds in front of the house are delineated with buxus hedging with granite cobble edges. The rear garden is delightful. The slender pond provides visual focus and a well-defined axis to a well-tended circular lawn. The garden is laid out with neatly clipped buxus hedging in keeping with the overall pristine nature of this home careful not to overpower but rather compliment the architectural detail of the house itself with its delicate decorative stonework, curved details of the windows with their gently arching stone frames and the magnificent bricked back. Further complemented by copper beach hedging, acers and hydrangea for maximum impact. A granite patio area with steps to the lawn provides the perfect terrace for outdoor dining and leisure time and maximises an enviable west facing orientation. There are two generous side accesses to the garden with a further patio area on the east side serving the mews.\n\nTHE MEWS: To the side of the house discreetly sits a modern detached two bedroom mews. The accommodation extends approx. 162sqm/1,752sqft sqft and comprises of a large open plan living / dining / kitchen area with breakfast counter worktop and divide. This large living area has a glazed wall to one side enjoying magnificent views of the garden. There are two bedrooms, one ensuite and the other which connects to the main bathroom. In addition there is a dressing room, separate utility room and cloaks closet. To the far end of the mews there is a large storage room providing excellent storage solution for household paraphernalia. The mews enjoys its own private, sheltered paved patio area. It provides much scope and food for thought as you contemplate how to make this wonderful house your own.\n\nLOCATION - A Sense of Connection: In many ways, Richmond Avenue South is one of the city s best kept secrets, simply because houses here so seldom come to market. So imagine the peace of a large, gracious and exceptionally well built house, set well back from the road, surrounded by trees, and bird song, all just minutes from the city centre. Imagine a riverbank walk on your doorstep, offering an hour s loop along winding paths, passing coffee stops should you fancy refreshment. Imagine a charming and friendly community, and excellent schools just moments away. And most of all, imagine the peaceful sense of a secure and beautiful home that has stood for more than a century, ready to walk into and welcome you today.\n\nSchools, including Gonzaga and Alexandra Colleges are close by, while an excellent array of artisan caf s and restaurants are a short walk, whether you head for Rathgar, Upper Rathmines, or further down to Ranelagh.\n\nThe community is established, yet welcoming, as neighbours may be met on the beautiful Dodder valley river walk, which can eat up an hour s exercise as you follow the path to Clonskeagh. Or take the Luas, successor to that famous first Dublin tramline, and find yourself at St Stephen s Green in under ten minutes.\n\nThe parks and the river, the quiet presence of Hatherton and its sister houses in the neighbourhood, andthe established beauty of old stone and mature trees all make this a unique area. It is a stretch of green space and tranquillity that is not urban, and yet not suburban. The best of both worlds: Hatherton, a country house on the edge of the city; and a rare chance to make it your own.
