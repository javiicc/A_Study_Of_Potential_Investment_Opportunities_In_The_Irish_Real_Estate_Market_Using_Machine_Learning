    Description

    • Sale Type: For Sale by Private Treaty
    • Overall Floor Area: 0.3 ac
    Roseville comprises a beautiful authentic Irish thatched cottage in lovely rural surroundings and within easy walking distance of Lusk village. The cottage is a listed building and stands on a site of c.3000 sq m or about one-third of an acre.\r\n\r\nThe accommodation, which is in need of COMPLETE REFURBISHMENT, comprises a living room, two bedrooms and a kitchen. There are also a number of outhouses to the rear. Water and electricity are on site.\r\n\r\nWe would like to draw your attention to the following information:\r\n\r\na)\t- This a PROTECTED STRUCTURE and all proposed changes to the building have to be agreed with FCC Conservation Officer. Aspiring buyers need to ascertain what this entails themselves and we have attached information leaflet from Fingal County Council with the relevant contact details.\r\n\r\nb)\t- There is NO SEWERAGE on site so buyers will need to apply for planning permission for a mini treatment works (Bio Cycle or sim). This normally requires a minimum site of half an acre and this site is only one-third of an acre. The site size could probably be overcome but it is another potential complication.\r\n\r\nc)\tIf a bank is involved they will probably insist on there being a PLANNING PERMISSION for the Bio Cycle in place as well as a schedule of works agreed with the Conservation Officer, costed and approved by the buyers architect, before advancing a mortgage. So all buyers needing to raise a mortgage will need to confirm this in advance with their banks.\r\n\r\n\r\nThe site / garden area is mainly located to the rear of the cottage with a great south-westerly aspect and mature surroundings. There is a side entrance as well as a small front garden with traditional railings separating it from the footpath.\r\n\r\nLusk village centre, schools, sports facilities are all within about 1200m of the house with a good footpath all the way into the village. Lusk also has excellent bus and rail links to the city centre as well as being just 10-15 drive of the M1, M50 Swords and Dublin airport.
