    Description\nLickaun Lodge is a luxurious tranquil property set on an elevated site which commands panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. Built in 2003 by skilled / artistic tradesmen using high end natural materials. A variety of wood, cedar, elm, ash and larch were carefully selected, dried and carved beams, decorative fascia and furniture. The main residence commands an approximate gross internal floor area of 130 sq. mts. adjoining stable block (ground and first floor accommodation) of 120 sq mts. \n\n\nThe detail and finish of the main residence were individually selected or made from natural material and integrated with the theme of individual rooms and house as a whole. Front window sills and surround are finished with hand cut limestone. The entrance porch with its outer natural stone feature and tiled floor sets the theme. The elegant cosy sitting room\xe2\x80\x99s main feature is the large limestone grey stone fireplace with its large wood burning stove. The Victorian style furniture and pitch pine hand carved staircase and matching floor boards; antique style light shades and switches compliment the room and provide a soft and relaxing environment. The kitchen area is finished with hand cut larch beams and timber ceiling. Kitchen units are solid timber units with a large Belfast sink. The gas/electric cooker sits in the heart of the kitchen with a beautiful marble background inscribed with \xe2\x80\x98Failte\xe2\x80\x99. The welcoming porch as its known is adjacent to the kitchen and provides additional storage space for everyday use. The handmade presses and decorative centre piece add a touch of warmth. The Sun Room with its Gothic themed high ceiling, larch beams and wrought iron finials provide a relaxing and spacious room with panoramic views of the front gardens and surrounding countryside.\n\nThe bedroom accommodation consists of a downstairs double bedroom with a large en-suite and decorated with toile de jouy fabric. The two first floor bedrooms are tastefully decorated and complimented by a bright bathroom with a deep bath, wooden wainscoting and chain cistern system.\nCentral heating is provided by an outside boiler, double glazed windows and well insulated attic provides ample heat. Outside lighting is excellent and complete privacy is assured. A modern alarm system was installed two years ago. This unique property is complemented by its mature and extensive gardens which have many facets, lavender scented beds, rock roses, rich mature trees, alpine scree beds, vegetable plots, fruit trees and a variety of berries for your winter preserves. The three cut stone patios provide seating areas for different times of the day, to enjoy morning or afternoon drink in complete privacy. \nThe adjoining stable block has three large stables, wooden ceilings; three layered stable doors and recessed lighting decorated with brick and cut stone, office / dining area and rest rooms / games rooms on the first floor. The laundry and drying rooms are located to the rear of the stable buildings. From the cut stone path to the yard, the large sand arena with post and rail fencing provides adequate exercise and jumping for the houses. Four large flood lights make winter work easy in the arena. The back stable block / store (70 Sq. Mts) have two large stables as well as a mare and foal stable unit and ample storage room. This would make a perfect haven for a unique summer home, equine enthusiast, nature lovers, composers, writers etc. Its central location close to all amenities, up-to-date internet service and well finished hand crafted work make it a comfortable, stress free place to live.\n\nViewing strongly recommended and strictly by appointment with Sole Selling Agent.\n\nFeatures\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Luxurious tranquil property set at rear of elevated site \nto maximise privacy. \n\xe2\x80\xa2 High end specification with unique finish. \n\xe2\x80\xa2 Detached stales with overhead accommodation. \n\xe2\x80\xa2 Large sand ring. \n\xe2\x80\xa2 Gravel Driveway and large back yard.\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Mature gardens with variation of plants, shrubs and trees \n\xe2\x80\xa2 Natural stone footpaths surrounding House and Stables\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Natural Slate Roof\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Hand Cut Limestone Window Sills\n\nBER Details\nBER: D1 BER No.111218509 Energy Performance Indicator:245.83 kWh/m\xc2\xb2/yr\n\nAccommodation\nEntrance Porch 1.40 m x 1.15 m\nTiled floor \n\nSitting Room 5.00 m x 4.10 m \nSolid wood floor, timber ceiling, solid fuel range with natural stone surrounds\n\nKitchen 5.48 m x 5.18 m\nSolid timber fitted units with Belfast sink, tiled floor, timber ceiling with \nexposed timber larch beams.
