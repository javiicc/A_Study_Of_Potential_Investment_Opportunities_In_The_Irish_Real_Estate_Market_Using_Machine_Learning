    Regina House is a three-bay three-storey over basement Georgian house, built c.1760 and located in the centre of the village of Slane known as The Square and part of the four large landmark Georgian houses known as The Four Sisters . Regina House retains many of its original features liked the hipped slate roof with decorative red brick chimneystacks, ashlar quoins, squared limestone walls with limestone plinth and eaves courses, limestone sills, up/down timber sash windows, internal decorative cornicing, stone door surround with a decorative keystone, plain glazed fanlight, timber paneled door and many more. The property extending to Approx. 315m /3400ft enjoys its own private access to the rear yard and outbuilding together with a large private tree lined south-west facing rear garden. Slane is an idyllic village steeped in Irish history located in a beautiful part of County Meath in the heart of the Boyne Valley. The property lends itself to being a very enviable family home or suitable for conversion to commercial/office use subject to Planning Permission. Located along the N2 and centrally between the towns of Drogheda and Navan makes it a very convenient property and less than a 30 minute drive from both the M50 orbital motorway and Dublin International Airport. Not often do such iconic buildings full of original features and character come to the market and not an opportunity to be missed.
