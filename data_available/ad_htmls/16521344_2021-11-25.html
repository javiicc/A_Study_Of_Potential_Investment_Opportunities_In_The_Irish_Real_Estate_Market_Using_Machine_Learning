    This fine 'du Moulin' built semi detached family home is ideally positioned on the cul de sac of Sycamore Road and enjoys a sunny south-west facing rear garden stretching to approximately 43 metres long (12m wide) and backing onto similarly large gardens on The Rise. With no through traffic and on a very wide road, the property benefits from a private aspect to the front with good off and on street car parking. The accommodation of approx. 125sqm excluding the garage has been well cared for over and in the same family since new. Overall the accommodation briefly comprises: Entrance hallway, living room, dining room, breakfast room, kitchen, downstairs showerroom, landing, four bedroom, a bathroom and separate wc. There is also a garage with access from the driveway and in turn gives access to the rear garden. \n\nSycamore Road is a highly convenient and sought after location in Mount Merrion within a short stroll of neighbourhood shops which are a convenience for residents.\xc2\xa0 \xc2\xa0There is a strong sense of a vibrant local community with an active residents association and Sycamore Road is within the Parish of St. Th\xc3\xa9r\xc3\xa8se and, as a result, within the catchment area for applicants to Scoil San Treasa. The property is also within an easy commute of many other local schools including Sion Hill, Mount Anville Primary and Secondary, Colaiste Eoin and Scoil Iosagain, Blackrock College, St. Andrews, St. Michael's College and Oatlands College to name a few. There are many sporting and leisure facilities with Deer Park on the doorstep and UCD campus is within a short stroll. The Community Centre is close by and the property is also within a short walk of the quality bus corridor on the N11 and the Aircoach with local shopping centers in Stillorgan, Dundrum and Blackrock.\n\nJohn du Moulin as a builder is a name synonymous with Mount Merrion together with John Kenny. Between them they were involved in the building of most of the homes around Mount Merrion and in 1952, John du Moulin secured the contract to build the Church of St Therese which is very closeby.\n\nHall\xc2\xa0 \xc2\xa0 With accommodation off and stairs to first floor level.\n\nLiving Room 5.35m x 3.6m.
