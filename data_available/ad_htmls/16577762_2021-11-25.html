    Stunning two bedroom mid terrace period home with a spacious attic conversion comes to the market finished to a high standard. \n \nThe house is nicely situated on Woodfield terrace, set back from the road. An ornate gate opens up to a thriving kitchen garden and pathway up to the front door. The front door forms part of an attractive fa\xc3\xa9\xc2\xa7ade with brick arches that surround high efficiency windows. On entering there is a welcoming hallway that is tiled with Victorian styled tiles. This leads through to the dining area featuring oak laminate floors and an antique cast iron fireplace complete with decorative tiling. An archway leads through to a comfortable living space. The laminate floors are continued through to this area which features a Stanley solid fuel stove that is flanked with alcove shelving. To the other side of the dining area is the stylish kitchen extension, which is well lit by two Velux windows. The kitchen features; a Belfast style sink, solid wood countertops, subway tiling, integrated appliances and a breakfast bar. Between the kitchen and the bathroom is a utility room that accommodates additional storage, washing appliances and a guest toilet. The sunny back garden is nicely landscaped with a patio that is ideal for entertaining and a lawned area that is complimented by attractive planting. A pathway leads up to the spacious garage, which measures 40sqm and is wired and plumbed. There is garage doors which open out to the parking and bin storage at the rear of the property. The garage has potential for a number of uses. The first floor accommodates two double bedrooms and the main bathroom. The master bedroom features the same oak floors and antique radiators that are on the ground floor. The floors are continued into the second bedroom which feature built in wardrobes similar to those found in the master bedroom. The main bathroom is well finished with subway tiling, a heated towel rail and separate bath and shower units. Stairs lead up to the light filled attic conversion, that accommodates two separate work spaces and an ensuite bathroom. This room features oak flooring and Velux windows. The ensuite is similar in style to the main bathroom, featuring subway tiles, high spec sanitary ware, a heated towel rail and shower unit. Plantation shutters in the living room, bedrooms and landing are another feature of this beautifully presented home.\n\n6 Woodfield Terrace is ideally situated where Kilmainham meets Inchicore and close to a host of wonderful independent coffee shops and food retailers. Close by are some of the City's most popular amenities, namely; The Memorial Gardens, the Royal Hospital Kilmainham and the Phoenix Park. The area is well connected by public transport, with a frequent bus service to the city centre close to the house and a Dublin bike station nearby. For longer journeys, the N4 and M50 can be quickly accessed.
