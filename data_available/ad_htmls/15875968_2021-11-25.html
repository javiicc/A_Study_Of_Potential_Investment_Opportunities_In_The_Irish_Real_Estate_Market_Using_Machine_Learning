    A truly remarkable 4 bed period residence incorporating a delightful stone-built hunting lodge (partly renovated, but requiring considerable outlay to bring to a completed stage) together the Coach house which is a self- contained 4 bed residence and stone-built outbuildings. \n\nDating back to the circa 1720, this property oozes charm and character. It is set in a beautifully elevated 0.8ha (2 acre) site enjoying captivating views of Lough Derg and is approached by a sweeping tree lined avenue. \n\nThis property is quietly tucked away enjoying a beautiful rural aspect, yet is situated within 1.5km of Two-Mile Gate Activity Centre and 5.5kms of Killaloe. The Hunting Lodge has been partly renovated and is ideally suitable as a home office or Commercial use (subject to planning permission). Ballycuggaran House is situated in an area where equestrian activities are very popular and with the added advantage of easy access to Lough Derg where sailing, fishing, kayaking etc are very well catered for. \n\nAlso close by, Killaloe known as the birth place of Brian Boru (High King of Ireland c.941-1014). Whilst he was High King, Brian Boru ruled from his native town of Killaloe making it the one time capital of Ireland. One of Ireland s best kept secrets, Killaloe/Ballina which are divided by the river Shannon (longest waterway in Ireland and navigable for 160 kms) is renowned for its host of amenities including boutiques, traditional pubs and fine dining. Killaloe/ Ballina also offers excellent local schools - Ballina Primary, Killaloe Boy s national school and St. Anne s Community College are sure to impress. The historic town also offers a Sunday Farmers market which offers an abundance of local produce.\n\nLimerick is also just a 20 minute drive away with the University of Limerick, Plassey Technology park all the City has to offer. A chance to catch a show at the University s concert hall or excellent shopping experience in the city centre or Crescent shopping centre. Shannon Airport is only 40 minutes drive away with daily flights to the U.K. Central Europe and the U.S.\n\nFurther Information\nCarefully attention has been directed to the works that have been carried out to the main dwelling house and these the installation of new windows, re-wiring, installation of a new heating system\nand redecoration. \n\nLocation \nKillaloe/Ballina 5.5km\nLimerick 29km\nShannon Airport 50km\nDublin 187km\n\n\nHunting Lodge\n\nEntrance Hall 7.3m x 3.0m.\n\nLiving Room 7.25m x 5.3m. with Inglenook fireplace\n\nKitchen/Dining Room 9.0m x 3.0m. with beamed ceiling\n\nBedroom One 4.4m x 3.5m.\n\nBedroom Two 8.2m x 4.6m. with ornate fireplace\n\nBedroom Three 4.7m x 3.6m. with ornate fireplace\n\nUpstairs landing\n\nBedroom Four 13.25m x 5.0m.\n\nThe Coach House\n\nEntrance Hall 2.5m x 2.0m. with ceramic tiled and hardwood floor, opening leading to kitchen/dining room\n\nKitchen/Dining Room 4.5m x 3.0m. with country style fitted kitchen and hardwood floor\n\nUtility Room 2.6m x 2.5m. plumbed\n\nLiving /Dining Room 8.7m x 4.5m. with solid fuel stove, hardwood flooring and French doors leading to exterior\n\nBedroom One 4.5m x 4.2m. with hardwood flooring and French doors to exterior\n\nUpstairs\n\nLanding 5.0m x 2.5m.\n\nBedroom Two 4.7m x 3.23m.\n\nBedroom Three 4.7m x 3.5m.\n\nBedroom Four 5.7m x 4.8m. with recessed lighting\n\nBathroom 2.75m x 2.0m. with bath, electric shower, wc and hand basin\n\n\nDIRECTIONS:\nProceed to Killaloe town.
