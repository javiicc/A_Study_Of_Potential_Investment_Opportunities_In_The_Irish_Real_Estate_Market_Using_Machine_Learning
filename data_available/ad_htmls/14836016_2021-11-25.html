    ***Deposit taken***\r\n\r\nGalvin Property are delighted to present No 23 Martin Street, Portobello to the market. A warm and inviting two bedroom mid terrace red brick which has been extended to the rear. The current owners have refurbished the property and the interior design combines the old and new seamlessly. \r\n\r\nThe attention to detail and quality of finish all through this beautiful home is obvious the moment you walk in the hall door. There are high grade wide board wooden floors throughout the ground floor with a separate living room the front of the house which provides a quiet sanctuary to enjoy a good read, the latest Netflix series or alternatively as a space to work from home. The open plan kitchen / dining room has a west facing aspect and also benefits from additional light from the vaulted ceiling and skylight over the stairwell with a wood burning stove as the focal point and there is plenty of space to dine. The extension allows for additional dining space and a separate utility to the rear behind closed doors. Sliding glass doors lead to solid wood timber deck at the back of the house which enjoys the afternoon and evening sun ideal for summer dining or entertaining friends over a BBQ when the weather permits. A wrought iron spiral staircase leads to the first floor where there are two bedrooms including the master room which is the full width of the front of the house and the well proportioned main bathroom.\r\n\r\nPortobello is distinctly popular due to it's proximity to the city centre coupled with the local amenities and a real sense of community. Martin Street which has become increasingly popular with working professionals in recent times is situated between the South Circular Road and Portobello Road close to the banks of the Grand Canal. Easily accessible and close to a host transport facilities including the LUAS, just 1.3km to St Stephens Green (about 15 minutes on foot). This two bedroom period property is tucked away from the hustle and bustle of the city yet within walking distance of Dublin's central business district and the IT Hub on Barrow St home to Google, Facebook and Twitters European Headquarters.
