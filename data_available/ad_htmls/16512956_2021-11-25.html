    Historic Irish country residence set in c. 5 acres of beautiful gardens and parkland with impressive stone cut courtyard buildings. \nThis unique manor house originally dates from the mid 17th century with an original early 1700 s Georgian house having a substantial elegant Victorian addition in the 1860 s.\nThe vast accommodation extends to 649.72sq.m/6994sq.ft approx.\nThe house is accessed through a wall and gated entrance with a sweeping oak tree bordered driveway leading to an impressive granite stepped entrance portico and an unusual elliptical fanlight. \nThe large entrance hall with pristine ceiling plasterwork and beautifully polished wooden floors leads to an elegant drawing room with an original marble fireplace and to a formal dining room with original fireplace. The entrance hall leads from the Victorian period directly to the Georgian and In a few short steps one moves back 150 years to an early Georgian period property complete with an original staircase and the classical lug doors of the period. The kitchen including a cast iron range cooker and a sitting/reading room with a Kilkenny limestone fireplace, a shower room and bathroom complete the downstairs accommodation.\nUpstairs there are six bedrooms and a very large landing and a house bathroom. Two of the bedrooms are en-suite. \nThe basement level benefits from being a half-basement so has significant natural light and direct access to the courtyard. Currently laid out with a wine cellar, bar room and a variety of storage rooms, it offers huge potential.\nThe formal gardens are well maintained with mature trees, shrubs and a large lawned area. There is a large fenced paddock area to the rear with a mill stream running through it and the original 18th century well.\nHistorical research by the current owners has allowed a compilation of all the occupants and owners since the 1650 s tracing back to Col. Robert Morgan MP for Sligo and a cavalry commander in Cromwell s western command. During famine times the estate was owned by a Col. Thomas.\nJones who made enormous efforts to feed the local population and built a famine wall around the estate. The original famine pot remains in the courtyard.\nThe property has been approved for a section 482 allowing the owner to offset costs incurred in repair, maintenance or restoration of the buildings or the garden.\nLocated close to the wild atlantic way in the heart of an area of outstanding natural beauty only 10km from Ballina and 6 km from the popular seaside town of Enniscrone. There are excellent local schools, GAA sports facilities and amenities including surfing, golf and horse riding.\n\nFolio Number SL921 F\n\nViewing highly recommended to really appreciate this
