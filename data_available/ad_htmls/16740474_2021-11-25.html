    You'd have to feel privileged getting the opportunity to market and sell Number 26 Wolfe Tone Street. The specification finish to No. 26 is every bit as good as it appears. No. 26 Wolfe Tone Street has had a full and complete refurbishment to include an almost full rebuild, with only the front facade left remaining. It's basically a new build.\r\nWolfe Tone Street is one of the original inner-city streets and holds great memories for Kilkenny people, of the location of the world renowned Padmore and Barnes boot factory.\r\nWolfe Tone Street is a two-minute stroll to Kilkenny's railway station and a five-minute stroll to Kilkenny City Centre.\r\nThe location is close to schools and shops and all of Kilkenny City's amenities. No. 26 Wolfe Tone Street has private parking to the rear and a large rear garden that faces southwest, it couldn't get better.\r\nStepping into No. 26 is stepping into perfection. Everything about the completed finish is just so useable and correct. The layout is for open plan living and total enjoyment of the full space. The house has been extended to the rear and the internal walls removed and replaced, it an amazing inner-city property.\r\nThere's a small reception hall as you enter and once inside the house just opens up!! A new heating system, with the ground floor having underfloor heating guarantees a warm snug house. A sliding door to one side does divide or open the ground floor room to the left as you enter. The full interior of this house is new. Walls, electrics, plumbing, stairs, everything you can see. The house has been insulated to the last and not one further euro will be required to move into No. 26 Wolfe Tone Street.\r\nThe layout as the attached floorplan and video show is ideal. High ceilings give a great feeling as you walk through. The specification on the kitchen, with the matt doors, stone worktop and contrasting subway tiles is pure class.\r\nAll the internal doors are Irish made with modern finishing's. The stairs are local made and all the crafts people that worked on the house were local. The bathroom finishes are amazing, and a pump water pressure system was installed. Every conceivable effort was made for the use of space and storage leaving it impossible to describe the level of finish achieved.\r\nThe whole house is full of light and the use of an upside-down blind system is very clever, controlling the sun and privacy. A downstairs bathroom with panelled walls and a featured subway tiled wall is an amazing bathroom finish.\r\nIt's incredible the space that No. 26 offers upstairs. There are 3 bedrooms on the first floor and still with potential for the 4th on the ground floor. The quality of the finish never falters, if anything it improves. The bathrooms are incredible, with the tiling of the ensuite deserving a special mention.\r\nIt's an experience to walk through this property and well deserving the accolades it will receive. The windows are sash to the front and double glazed throughout. There's loads of space to the rear of No. 26 Wolfe Tone Street, with electricity and water brought down the garden. There's private parking and lawned areas.\r\nNo. 26 Wolfe Tone Street gives the ultimate inner city living experience. The property has everything you could desire and a specification finish to match.
